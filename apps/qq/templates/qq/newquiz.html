About

The OED contains over three million quotations illustrating English usage throughout its history. Although a typical quotation may only be fifteen or twenty words long, there are often several clues to a quotation's date: vocabulary, spelling, conventions of capitalization or punctuation, the topic of the quotation, and references to people or events. If you're lucky (or extremely well-read), you may even recognize some of the passages.

How to play

Quotations are picked at random, and may be from any year between 1500 and 2000. If you can't guess the date for a particular quotation, you can skip it and try another. But you are only allowed three skips per game.
You start with 100 points. You win points if you guess within 10 years of the correct date. You lose points if you are more than 10 years out; the further out you are, the more points you lose.
Get the date exactly right to win a bonus of 50 points.
The game ends when you run out of points.

Links to OED Online

When the date and source of each quotation is revealed, this includes a link back to its entry in OED Online. You will need an OED Online subscription in order to view the entry.

Why no pre-1500 quotations?

The OED includes quotation evidence from the whole written history of English, including Old English and Middle English manuscript sources. But many of these early sources are hard to date exactly, so are not suitable for this game. (We've also omitted any post-1500 quotations which have approximate or speculative dates.)
